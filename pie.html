Apple π

Ingredients

  • apples, 500 g
  • sugar, 50 g
  • raisins
  • lime juice
  • cinnamon
  • flour, 100 g
  • butter, 75 g
  • brown sugar, 60 g
  • 1/4 egg
  • salt

Instructions

Preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celcius. Get a pie pan, dough-roller and put on some music. I recommend the beautiful Jim Guthrie's Takes Time.

Here we go.

To make the dough, add the flour, butter (sliced up), brown sugar and the bit of egg together in a bowl. Mix and knead quickly and shortly until there is a consistent texture. Use half the dough to form a ball and flatten it on a floured surface. Butter up the pie pan. Line the pie plate and trim the dough to fit. Use part of the remainder to line the edges of the pan.

By now you should be snacking on a bit of dough and enjoying the revelatory experience that is Never Poor.

To create the filling, dice up the apples and mix with the sugar. Add raisins, lime juice and cinnamon to taste. Fill the dough-lined pie pan up with this apple-goodness. From the remaining dough, create a few strips and layer them over the filling.

As moods settle while Don't be Torn transitions into The Sound of Wanting More, throw the pie in the oven, and let it sit there for about 50 minutes, and enjoy the rest of the album. Once that's done, don't be hasty; let it cool off before you slice it up and serve to you and yours.

Bon appetit, and enjoy Pi day with gusto.
